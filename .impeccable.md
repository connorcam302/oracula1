# Oracula — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
A private friend group of ~12 people tracking F1 fantasy results across seasons. These are **data nerds who love stats** — they dig into charts, compare efficiency ratings, and obsess over numbers. They use the app actively during race seasons to track standings and debate results.

### Brand Personality
**Bold. Precise. Energetic.**
Feels like a sports broadcast — punchy, fast, high-stakes. The energy of race day, not a corporate dashboard. Friends compete and trash-talk; the app should feel alive and charged, not sterile.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Dark mode primary** — carbon blacks with charged color accents
- **F1 Red (#E10600) as the dominant brand color** — used for primary actions, active nav, key CTAs
- **Championship Gold (amber/gold range)** — for P1 moments, podiums, champion crowning
- **Electric Blue (data/info)** — cool counterpart to red for charts, secondary data, info states
- Anti-references: generic gray SaaS dashboard, AI-slop purple gradients, overly corporate look

### Design Principles
1. **Color earns its place** — every color has a semantic role (red = action/brand, gold = excellence, blue = data/info)
2. **Data hierarchy first** — color should help users instantly understand rankings, states, and comparisons
3. **Sports broadcast energy** — bold, fast, confident; not timid or neutral
4. **Tint the neutrals** — no pure grays; all surfaces carry a subtle warm or cool tint for cohesion
5. **60-30-10 rule** — dark neutrals dominate, red accents key actions, gold and blue accent specific moments
